01 — Research Areas

Core Research Directions

From component dynamics to system-scale multi-objective optimization.

01
Energy System Modeling

High-fidelity dynamic models of integrated wind–solar–hydrogen–ammonia systems using equation-based and data-driven hybrid approaches.

02
Hydrogen Production Optimization

Electrolyzer stack modeling, operational strategy optimization, and degradation mitigation for PEM and alkaline electrolysis systems.

03
Ammonia Synthesis Dynamics

Transient response analysis and flexible operation scheduling of green ammonia reactors under variable renewable energy input.

04
Multi-scale Scheduling

Hierarchical scheduling integrating day-ahead planning, real-time dispatch, and MPC for grid-interactive energy hubs.

03 — Projects & Platform

Projects & Modeling Framework

Funded programs and the open-source simulation platform under development.

National Key R&D Program — Flexible Operation of Green Ammonia Systems
Multi-partner project on dynamic load-following strategies for Haber–Bosch loops powered by offshore wind. 2023–2027.
NSFC General Program — Hierarchical Energy Management for Hydrogen Hubs
MILP and MPC co-design frameworks for coupled electrolyzer–storage–grid assets under uncertainty. 2022–2025.
Provincial Science Foundation — Physics-informed ML for Electrolyzer Degradation
PINNs-based degradation prediction and adaptive control for long-duration PEM electrolysis. 2023–2026.
Industry Collaboration — Digital Twin for Wind–Solar–Storage Microgrid
Real-time digital twin with hardware-in-the-loop validation for a 10 MW demonstration site.
